The First Reach: Settlement Summary

At the opening of Book One, humanity has established 14 stations and habitats across the solar system — from Earth orbit to Saturn. The Space Elevator and Moon Fuel Station form the backbone of the transit network. The Intrepid is the symbolic heart: the first, the boldest, and still the most famous address in space. Every person living beyond Earth owes something to what was proven there.

The outer planets beyond Saturn remain untouched. The Belt is still developing. Mars is the only planet with multiple surface settlements. Saturn is the edge of the known world — and somewhere in its orbit or rings, Ella's mother was last seen.
